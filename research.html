Drought Research: A Global and Indian Perspective

Drought is one of the most devastating natural calamities that affects millions of people globally. It is a prolonged period of deficient rainfall that leads to water shortages, poor crop yields, and widespread economic distress. This research highlights the causes, impacts, and potential solutions for droughts, with a special focus on India and its growing challenges in managing droughts.

Global Impact of Drought

Global Drought Patterns

Droughts are a global issue that have become increasingly severe and frequent due to climate change. The United Nations reports that nearly 55 million people globally are affected by drought every year. The impacts range from water scarcity to food insecurity, and it is expected that droughts will become more frequent as the planet's climate changes.

India is one of the most drought-prone countries in the world, with over 50% of its landmass being vulnerable to drought conditions. The country experiences severe droughts, especially in the arid regions of Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Maharashtra. With over 600 million people dependent on agriculture, the agricultural sector is particularly vulnerable to droughts, affecting both food production and livelihoods.

Potential Solutions to Mitigate Drought Effects

Water Conservation and Technology

Solutions to combat drought include improving water conservation practices, implementing efficient irrigation technologies, and enhancing water storage capacity. In India, initiatives like rainwater harvesting and micro-irrigation systems are being promoted to improve water management in drought-affected areas. Globally, countries are adopting desalination technologies and water reuse practices to meet the growing water demand.
